Mission

A living laboratory, opened to everyone.

The iconic design of Biosphere 2 offers tremendous opportunities for research in earth and ecological sciences — paired with a unique approach to education and outreach that is intimately linked with cutting-edge research activities.

Visitors to Biosphere 2 don't read about science on a placard. They see science in action, interact with university research, and have hands-on experiences with Earth-systems science. Families, student groups, the university community, and the public can all experience Biosphere 2's innovative approach to tackling some of our biggest scientific questions.

What makes Biosphere 2 different from a museum, a science center, or a national park is that the education mission is not adjacent to the research — it is the research. Every public tour walks past active experiments. Every K-12 field trip meets a working scientist. Every REU fellow presents their findings not only at a symposium, but to 100,000 annual visitors who happen to be walking through.

Three Commitments

What our education and outreach programs do.

Biosphere 2's programs are sustainable and community-oriented — and they address fundamental scientific challenges regarding water and energy management in semi-arid environments.

01

Illuminate the coupling of life, energy, and water.

We highlight the complex coupling of life, energy, and water in the dynamics of our world — to inspire students and the general public, including K-12 teachers and their classrooms. Every program is grounded in the idea that Earth's systems are not separate subjects but one continuous story.

02

Train the next generation of science communicators.

We train leaders among university faculty and students in communicating science to the general public. From Carson Scholars to REU fellows, every researcher who passes through Biosphere 2 leaves with practice explaining their work to a fifth-grader, a retiree, and a Congressman — sometimes all in the same afternoon.

03

Be responsive to the community we serve.

We are responsive to the needs of diverse stakeholders and audiences — tribal nations, school districts, the University of Arizona community, and the global public who walk through our doors. Programs are designed with educators, not for them, and reshaped every year based on what teachers tell us worked.

Flagship Program

The REU — where research meets the public.

A unique part of the Biosphere 2 REU experience is the opportunity to interact with 100,000 public visitors and be trained in outreach related to each fellow's research topic. By the end of the summer, every fellow has presented their work to a fifth-grader, a retired engineer, and a journalist — sometimes all in the same afternoon.

The program is structured with five interrelated components: research practice, interdisciplinary context, real-world relevance, professional development, and science communication. Together they prepare fellows not just for graduate school, but for a career in which the public is a partner, not an audience.

Land Acknowledgment

We respectfully acknowledge the University of Arizona is on the land and territories of Indigenous peoples. Today, Arizona is home to 22 federally recognized tribes, with Tucson being home to the O'odham and the Yaqui. The university strives to build sustainable relationships with sovereign Native Nations and Indigenous communities through education offerings, partnerships, and community service.
