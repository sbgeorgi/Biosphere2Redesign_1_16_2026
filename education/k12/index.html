A Living Classroom

Science stories that walk with you, biome by biome.

Through the K-12 app, students and their teachers explore this advanced scientific research facility — the world's largest closed system with everything needed to support human life long-term. Through science stories you and your students will have the opportunity to learn more about our world-class research as you traverse the path around the exterior, through the human habitat, and into the interior wilderness areas of Biosphere 2.

The wilderness areas include the coastal fog desert, thorn scrub, savanna, and rainforest — each a working experiment in Earth-systems science, each with a story the app tells at the student's own pace.

Before You Get Here

Pre-visit prep, in three steps.

A great field trip starts the week before. Here's how to set your students up to get the most out of Biosphere 2.

Download the app

Install the Biosphere 2 K-12 app on every device students will use. Pre-load audio and video content at school — there's limited cell service on-site.

Watch & prime

Walk through one or two of Biosphere 2's YouTube stories together in class — the Coral Reef Lab and the Cyborg Forest (WALD Campaign) are great starting points.

Simulate

Run a SIMOC session in class. Let students design a closed-system habitat for Mars or the Moon, then compare their choices to what Biosphere 2 actually tried in 1991.

Bring questions

Every biome has a working scientist. Have each student write down one question they want to ask a researcher when they're on-site — we'll make sure they meet one.

Stories You'll Walk Through

Six biomes. Six science stories.

The K-12 app narrates each biome your students will visit. Here's a preview of what they'll hear, see, and feel.

Coastal Fog Desert ~78°F · arid

The fog-fed desert

One of the only fog-desert ecosystems on Earth enclosed under glass — a mirror of Baja California's coastal desert. Students learn how plants survive on moisture condensed from fog, and how this biome helps researchers understand arid-land agriculture.

Story stop 03
Mangrove Biome Brackish · saline

Where land meets sea

A living model of coastal mangrove forests — ecosystems that protect coastlines worldwide and store more carbon per acre than any terrestrial forest. The story stop explains how B2's mangroves are studied as a climate-resilience blueprint.

Story stop 06
Rainforest 85°F · 90% RH

The Cyborg Forest

Biosphere 2's rainforest is wired — sensors on every tree, sap-flow monitors in every trunk. Students learn about the WALD Campaign, a long-term study turning the rainforest into a living instrument that records its own response to drought and heat.

Story stop 08
Ocean Reef Lab Saltwater · 26°C

Coral in recovery

In 2024, living corals returned to Biosphere 2's ocean for the first time in more than 30 years. Students see the research team restoring a coral reef under glass — and learn why this small ocean holds lessons for the world's dying reefs.

Story stop 11
Savanna Warm · seasonal

The transition zone

A working model of the grassland-forest transition that covers much of Africa and Australia. Students learn how savannas flip between grassland and woodland depending on rainfall, fire, and herbivory — and what that means for our own semi-arid Southwest.

Story stop 05
Human Habitat Air-conditioned

Life inside Biosphere 2

The apartments, kitchen, and farm where the original eight Biospherians lived sealed inside for two years — 1991 to 1993. Students see where food was grown, medicine was made, and oxygen was tracked molecule by molecule. A story about what it takes to keep humans alive on a closed system.

Can't visit in person?

Two ways to bring Biosphere 2 into your classroom.

Free · Browser-based

SIMOC — model your own habitat

An agent-based simulation developed by the University of Arizona and the National Science Foundation. Students design closed-system habitats for Earth, Mars, or the Moon — choosing crops, atmosphere, crew size, and power — then watch their civilization thrive or collapse over simulated years.

Launch SIMOC
Virtual · Free for classrooms

Sessions with a Scientist

Hour-long virtual visits that connect Biosphere 2 researchers with K-12 classrooms anywhere on planet Earth. Free lessons focus on ocean and coral resilience, the effects of climate change on rainforests, and how Biosphere 2 provides a model for scientists to understand the future of our planet.

For Teachers

Frequently asked questions.

Groups of 15 or more receive discounted admission. Title I schools in Arizona may qualify for additional support — contact us at bio2-group@arizona.edu to discuss options. The K-12 app itself is free on both the App Store and Google Play.

The app-guided K-12 tour takes approximately 75 minutes and covers about one mile of walking, including roughly 100 stairs. We recommend budgeting at least 2 hours total to allow for arrival, bathroom breaks, lunch at the cafe, and time in the gift shop. Many classes stay for a half-day.

We recommend one adult chaperone per 8–10 students. The biomes have narrow walkways and stairs, so smaller sub-groups work best. Chaperones should also download the K-12 app so they can answer questions and help students find story stops.

Yes. The Biosphere 2 Cafe offers light refreshments and there are outdoor patio areas where school groups can eat packed lunches. Food and drinks are not permitted inside the biomes themselves — please plan to leave lunches on the bus or in designated eating areas.

Yes. The K-12 app's story stops map to Arizona K-12 science standards, particularly in Earth and Space Sciences, Life Sciences, and Engineering, Technology, and Applications of Science. A standards-alignment guide is available on request — email bio2-group@arizona.edu.

Strollers are not permitted in certain biome interior areas due to stairs and narrow passageways. Front-carriers and backpack child-carriers work well. The visitor center, cafe, and gift shop are fully stroller-accessible. See our accessibility page for full details.

Two great options. Sessions with a Scientist brings a Biosphere 2 researcher into your classroom virtually for an hour-long Q&A and demo — free for K-12 classrooms anywhere. SIMOC is a free browser-based simulator where students design their own closed-system habitats on Earth, Mars, or the Moon. Both are designed to stand alone or to prime students for an eventual in-person visit.
