Overview

Research, with the public as partner — not audience.

The Biosphere 2 REU provides undergraduates an opportunity to conduct guided research in environmental and Earth systems science at a leading and unique research institution. We are recruiting ten students for the 2026 summer.

Students receive stipends of $6,000 for the 10-week research internship, food allowance, housing on the Biosphere 2 campus, funding for travel to and from Tucson, AZ, as well as support for travel to a professional meeting to present their work. For 2026, the program runs from June 8th to August 14th. Students will be selected from across the country to participate in the program and they will conduct research at Biosphere 2.

The REU program at Biosphere 2 is supported by a grant from the National Science Foundation. We share the goals of the NSF to use the REU experience as a way to provide research opportunities for students who might not have such opportunities readily available at their home institutions. The summer program will allow students to gain experience in research firsthand, training and preparation for careers in science, and opportunities to communicate science to diverse audiences. Students will also work with partner organizations at the University of Arizona to connect research findings to applied environmental solutions. Participation in the REU program will help prepare students for graduate studies in science.

Program Structure

The program is structured with five interrelated components.

Each component is taught in parallel — not sequence — so fellows build research practice, scientific context, real-world relevance, professional development, and communication skills simultaneously.

01 · Practice

Individual research projects.

Fellows conduct their own research under a mentor, working on a defined question in environmental or Earth systems science — from hydrology and soil science to ecology, plant sciences, and computer science.

02 · Context

Earth-systems framework.

Fellows place their project within the broader interdisciplinary Earth-system sciences framework — understanding how their slice of the system fits into the coupled cycles of water, energy, and life.

03 · Relevance

Real-world applications.

Each project is framed by its relevance to environmental and societal challenges — fostering practical solutions, not just academic papers. Fellows work with University of Arizona partner organizations to connect findings to applied outcomes.

04 · Profession

Career development.

Professional development workshops, field trips to local attractions, and direct mentorship prepare fellows for the successful pursuit of Earth and environmental science careers — including graduate study.

05 · Communication

Public science communication.

Fellows present research findings in a formal symposium setting — and, uniquely, to the 100,000 public visitors who walk through Biosphere 2 each year. Trained in outreach, fellows learn to communicate the process, importance, and findings of research to broad audiences.

Eligibility

Am I eligible to apply?

The Biosphere 2 REU welcomes applicants from a wide range of disciplines. The four criteria below are NSF requirements; the rest is up to you.

Enrollment status

Students must be enrolled in a degree program and, following the REU experience, have at least one semester of college left before graduation. High school graduates who have been accepted at an undergraduate institution but who have not yet started their undergraduate study are also eligible to participate.

Citizenship

Students must be U.S. citizens or permanent residents to be eligible for NSF/REU grant support. NSF rules are firm on this point — we cannot make exceptions, even for strong applicants.

Field of study

Students majoring in, or in a curriculum leading to, one of the following fields will be well suited to participate: hydrology, soil science, geology, atmospheric science, biology, ecology, plant sciences, mathematics, physics, chemistry, or computer science.

Encouraged applicants

We encourage applications from students in groups underrepresented in science — including first-generation college students, transfer students, veterans, and students from institutions without dedicated undergraduate research programs.

How To Apply

Three pieces. Two surveys. One narrative.

Write your narrative

A 2-page maximum narrative statement covering your educational and career goals, research experiences, and research interests. PDF only. The narrative is the most important part of your application — give it time.

Identify two references

Two faculty members at your home institution (or recent research supervisors) who can speak to your abilities. Send them the reference survey link early — letters are required for a complete application.

Submit the Qualtrics survey

Complete the student application survey, uploading your narrative PDF and providing contact info for your two references.

Optionally, also apply via ETAP

Students can also apply on the NSF ETAP website. Applying through both channels maximizes your visibility to the selection committee.
