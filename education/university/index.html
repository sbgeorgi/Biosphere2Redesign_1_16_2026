Our Approach

From first-year seminars to doctoral dissertations.

Biosphere 2 is a site for many engaging undergraduate and graduate experiences for both UArizona students as well as students from other institutions. Programs run the full arc of a university career — from a first-year Honors internship to a senior Engineering Capstone to a multi-year VIP team membership that culminates in a thesis.

What unites them is access: to a 3.14-acre controlled environment, to working scientists, to the 100,000 visitors a year who walk through the same door researchers do. University programs at Biosphere 2 are not adjacent to research — they are the research.

For UArizona Students

Five programs built for the home team.

Current University of Arizona undergraduates and graduate students have five dedicated Biosphere 2 programs. Each is run in partnership with a UofA college, institute, or department — credit-bearing where appropriate.

Vertically Integrated Projects (VIP)

Multi-year Credit-bearing

A transformative approach to higher education that engages undergraduate and graduate students in ambitious, long-term, large-scale, multidisciplinary project teams led by faculty. Rigorously evaluated and refined over more than two decades.

Learn more

Honors College Internship

Honors only Funded

A hands-on experience for Franke Honors students to conduct funded research projects with the Biosphere 2 research teams. Honors students can also earn honors units through a Vertically Integrated Project while participating in research, design, and development related to Biosphere 3 — a digital twin of Biosphere 2.

Learn more

Faculty Innovative Teaching Fellows

Faculty Course dev

Faculty Innovative Teaching Fellows (FIT) connect Biosphere 2 opportunities to UArizona students by including Biosphere 2 in their existing courses or in development of new courses or sections. A fellowship for faculty who want to teach with the facility, not just about it.

Learn more

Engineering Capstone

Seniors Industry partner

A required senior capstone course that creates university-industry partnerships — allowing companies (and Biosphere 2 itself) to tackle pressing projects through motivated engineering students, and providing students with impactful real-world experience before graduation.

Learn more

Carson Scholars

Grad students Sci comm

A program dedicated to training the next generation of environmental graduate researchers in the art of public communication. Throughout the scholarship year, Carson Scholars work closely with faculty mentors who provide hands-on training to develop strategies to effectively convey the value of their research to a variety of audiences.

Learn more
For UofA & Visiting Students

Three programs open to everyone.

Three Biosphere 2 programs are open to students from any institution — including the flagship NSF-funded REU summer residency.

Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU)

Summer · 10 weeks $6,000 stipend Apply by Mar 1

A NSF-funded summer program providing ten undergraduates from across the country an opportunity to conduct guided research in environmental and earth systems science. Stipend, housing, travel, and conference travel included.

Explore REU

Environmental Science Institute

Multi-week Open institution

Provides undergraduate students an opportunity to conduct guided research in environmental and earth systems science at a leading and unique research institution. Designed for students who want to test-drive a research career before committing to graduate school.

Learn more

Undergraduate Internships

Academic year Research-team based

Undergraduate internships available through the research teams at Biosphere 2 — from the Landscape Evolution Observatory to the Ocean Reef Lab. Open to students at any institution; arranged directly with the research team whose work aligns with your interests.

VIP Spotlight

Current Biosphere 2 VIP teams.

The VIP program includes both standalone and interconnected projects. The Arizona Institutes for Resilience (AIR) and the Biosphere 2 Honors VIP groups are multi-project enterprises with a VIP team dedicated to each project. In the AIR VIP group, long-term goals include collaboration across teams to address overarching scientific questions.

Food · Energy · Water

Agrivoltaics: Food, Energy, and Water Solutions

Designing and studying the co-location of solar panels and agriculture — measuring how shade, water capture, and microclimate shift crop yield and energy production simultaneously.

Ocean · Climate

Coral Reef Resilience: Biosphere 2 Science at Scale

Studying the world's only enclosed ocean reef as a controlled-environment blueprint for coral restoration. Living corals returned to the B2 ocean in 2024 for the first time in 30+ years.

Data · Design

Innovative Data Visualization at Biosphere 2

Translating B2's torrent of sensor data — sap flow, soil moisture, atmospheric gas — into visual stories that scientists and the public can both act on.

Rainforest · Climate

Biosphere 2 Rainforest Resilience

Capturing and communicating ecosystem responses to climate change through the WALD Campaign — a long-term study turning B2's rainforest into a living instrument.

How to Join

Find your program, then contact the team directly.

Each Biosphere 2 university program has its own application process, timeline, and eligibility. Use the program links above to reach the team that runs each one — they will tell you what's currently open, what's coming, and what the next step looks like for a student with your background.

If you're not sure which program fits, the REU is the most accessible entry point for students from any institution — and its application is open through March 1, 2026.
