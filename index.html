Biomes.

Explore a 3.14-acre living laboratory where cutting-edge research unfolds across diverse ecosystems—including a rainforest, ocean, mangrove, desert, and the Landscape Evolution Observatory. Unlike in the natural world, scientists at Biosphere 2 can precisely control and isolate environmental variables, allowing them to study interactions that are impossible to separate in nature.

Scientific Capabilities.

Landscape Evolution (LEO)

The Landscape Evolution Observatory (LEO) is the world's largest controlled experiment on watershed hydrology. Three massive hillslopes filled with crushed basalt allow scientists to track every drop of water and trace how life shapes physical landscapes.

KEY RESEARCH: Subsurface water flow, Basalt weathering, Carbon sequestration. LEAD EXPERTISE: Dr. Peter Troch, Dr. Luke Pangle
