System Streams & Repositories

The data model used for Biosphere 2 data was developed and implemented as a relational database called SensorDB. It supports real-time monitoring of all data streams while archiving observations for visualization and publication.

Biosphere 2 was intended to serve as a tool for the scientific community. Researchers interested in conducting experiments at Biosphere 2 or utilizing its collected datasets are encouraged to complete the Data Interest Form and connect with our affiliate scientists.

Biosphere 2 Data Policy

Datasets collected from all research initiatives have limited access to Biosphere 2 investigators and authorized collaborators.

Data Access

To get permission to access Biosphere 2 datasets, please fill out the Data Interest Form or contact the relevant system lead:

  • Dr. Scott Saleska — LEO data
  • Dr. Joost van Haren — Tropical Rainforest data
  • Dr. Diane Thompson — Ocean Reef data
  • John Adams — Weather Station & SCADA data

Data Use & Quality

The user of Biosphere 2 data agrees not to resell or redistribute shared data. Users should be aware that these data are provisional and could change at any time. While efforts have been taken to ensure that these data are of the highest quality, there is no guarantee of perfection.

Proper Acknowledgment

The following acknowledgment should accompany any publication or citation of these datasets:

"Data for this study were provided by the [Landscape Evolution Observatory Project / Tropical Rain Forest / Ocean Reef Lab / Weather Station], Biosphere 2, University of Arizona."
