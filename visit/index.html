Welcome

A Living Laboratory in the Sonoran Desert

Biosphere 2 is a 3.14-acre, glass-enclosed research facility where scientists study Earth's ecosystems under controlled conditions. Originally built in 1991 as a closed-system experiment, it has evolved into a world-class research center operated by the University of Arizona.

Today, visitors from around the globe explore this engineering marvel through the Biosphere 2 Experience—an app-guided audio tour that takes you through the human habitat, wilderness biomes, and behind the scenes of cutting-edge science.

Before You Arrive

Tips for Your Visit

Recommended

  • Wear comfortable, closed-toe walking shoes
  • Bring a water bottle—staying hydrated in the desert is essential
  • Download the Biosphere 2 Experience app before arriving
  • Bring earbuds or headphones for the audio tour
  • Wear sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
  • Arrive by 2:00 PM for the full experience
  • Charge your phone—the app uses video and audio

Please Avoid

  • High heels or open-toe sandals (stairs involved)
  • Large bags or backpacks in tight biome spaces
  • Food or drinks inside the biomes
  • Touching plants or research equipment
  • Pets (service animals welcome—see Accessibility)
  • Smoking anywhere on the grounds
  • Drones or unauthorized photography equipment
